# Messaging and Homepage Copy

This document defines the product messaging foundation for Postgresome.

## Messaging principles

1. Lead with diagnosis, not charts.
2. Lead with developer pain, not generic observability words.
3. Make the value concrete: faster root-cause analysis.
4. Show evidence and trust, not vague automation claims.

## Positioning line

Postgresome is a PostgreSQL diagnosis assistant for engineering teams.

## Headline options

### Option 1

Your PostgreSQL doctor.

### Option 2

Find what is wrong with Postgres before it becomes an outage.

### Option 3

PostgreSQL diagnosis for backend teams.

### Option 4

Know what changed, why it matters, and what to fix next.

## Primary subheadline

Postgresome analyzes your PostgreSQL behavior, detects real problems, explains
the cause with evidence, and recommends the next step.

## Supporting proof line

Works across RDS, Cloud SQL, Supabase, Neon, and self-hosted PostgreSQL.

## Core value blocks

### Detect real issues

Postgresome surfaces meaningful findings like lock contention, vacuum lag,
missing indexes, slow query regressions, and bloat risk.

### Explain why they are happening

Every diagnosis includes evidence, historical context, and likely cause so
engineers do not have to decode raw PostgreSQL signals by hand.

### Fix faster

Move from symptom to action with clear next steps tied to the actual database
problem.

## Website section ideas

### Section: The problem

You already have metrics. You still lose time figuring out what they mean.

### Section: The workflow

Setup -> Diagnosis -> Evidence -> Action

### Section: Example finding

Show:

- title
- explanation
- evidence bullets
- impact
- recommended next step

### Section: Built for engineering teams

- diagnosis-first
- cross-provider
- historical reasoning
- evidence-backed recommendations

## Messaging to avoid

Avoid phrases like:

- full-stack observability
- AI database copilot
- all your metrics in one place
- dashboards for everything

Those either sound generic or move the product away from its strongest wedge.
